This is the audio page

Audio refers to sound, especially when recorded, transmitted, or reproduced. It encompasses various aspects: Sound Waves: Audio is fundamentally based on sound waves, which are vibrations that travel through the air (or other mediums) and are detected by our ears. Analog vs. Digital: Analog audio captures sound waves in their continuous form. Examples include vinyl records and cassette tapes. Digital audio converts sound waves into a series of numerical values. Common digital formats include MP3, WAV, and AAC. Recording: Audio can be captured using microphones, which convert sound waves into electrical signals. These signals can then be stored on various media. Playback: Audio playback devices, like speakers and headphones, convert stored audio signals back into sound waves that we can hear. Editing and Mixing: Digital audio workstations (DAWs) allow for detailed editing and mixing of audio tracks, enabling the creation of complex soundscapes and music. Transmission: Audio can be transmitted over different media, including radio waves, streaming over the internet, and Bluetooth. Quality: The quality of audio can be influenced by factors such as bit rate in digital recordings, the quality of the recording equipment, and the acoustics of the environment. Audio is integral to many forms of media and communication, from music and podcasts to movies and video games.
